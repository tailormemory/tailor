# Extraction Quality Comparison — Fact Extraction Backend Decision

**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Context**: Decision input for `enrichment.fact_extraction.backends` ordering in `config/tailor.yaml`. Question: should Anthropic Haiku 4.5 remain primary, or should Gemini Flash 2.5 take over for cost reasons (~10× cheaper)?

## Methodology

- Sample: 30 chunks from production ChromaDB collection `tailor_kb`
  - 10 email
  - 10 conversation (5 chatgpt + 5 claude)
  - 10 document
- Each chunk processed by 3 models with **identical prompt** (`EXTRACTION_PROMPT` from `scripts/enrichment/extract_facts_nightly.py:70`)
- Models: `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`, `gemini-2.5-flash`, `gpt-4o-mini`
- Output: JSON array of facts with category + entity_tags + event_date
- Same temperature (0.0), same max_tokens (2000)

## Aggregate Results

| Metric | Haiku | Gemini Flash 2.5 | GPT-4o-mini |
|--------|-------|------------------|-------------|
| Total facts extracted | 256 | 272 | 239 |
| Average per chunk | 8.5 | 9.1 | 8.0 |
| Avg latency | 3.3s | 3.5s | 7.7s |
| **JSON parse failures** | **0/30** | **4/30** | **0/30** |
| Cost relative to Haiku | 1× (baseline) | ~0.1× | ~0.3× |

## Qualitative Findings

### Haiku 4.5 — strengths
- Zero parse failures
- Best filler suppression: respects "Extract ONLY factual statements, not filler" constraint reliably
- Good aggregation: combines related nutritional/numeric facts into single readable statements rather than fragmenting
- Most concise factual phrasing in italian ("DOCUSIGN ha effettuato un pagamento di 90€" rather than verbose alternatives)

### Haiku 4.5 — weaknesses
- Slight under-extraction on dense numeric documents (missed 2 transactions on extracted-conto sample vs Gemini)
- Sometimes drops temporal precision when aggregating

### Gemini Flash 2.5 — strengths
- Higher recall on structured numeric data (transactions, financial breakdowns)
- More granular fact emission

### Gemini Flash 2.5 — weaknesses (BLOCKERS)
- **13% parse failure rate** (4/30): mix of 503 Service Unavailable, truncated JSON, and one silent zero-array on valid input
- Extracts retorical/banal "facts" that violate the prompt's filler-suppression rule (examples: "È necessario un leader con la L maiuscola", "Le opportunità sono enormi per chi ha idee, visione, competenza")
- Verbose phrasing: "Il documento registra un'operazione con ID 1271, tipo BP, del 04/03/2016, con un importo di 5.24" instead of action-shaped facts
- Fragment-based: emits "0.4g grassi saturi" as separate fact rather than aggregating into the parent breakdown

### GPT-4o-mini — neutral
- Quality comparable to Haiku, never best, never worst
- 2.3× slower than Haiku (7.7s vs 3.3s avg) — would add ~3 hours to nightly pipeline at 2,500 chunks/night
- Useful as 3rd-tier fallback, not as primary

## Decision

**Anthropic Haiku 4.5 remains the primary backend for `fact_extraction`.**

Reasons:
1. **Reliability**: 0% parse failure vs Gemini's 13% under realistic prompt + content load. On 2,500 chunks/night, 13% failure means ~325 chunks routed to fallback, eroding the cost-saving rationale.
2. **Prompt adherence**: Haiku respects "no filler" constraint; Gemini emits retorical fragments that pollute the KB and create downstream noise in supersession.
3. **Aggregation quality**: Haiku produces facts at the right granularity (action + subject); Gemini fragments numeric data, multiplying supersession workload for no semantic gain.

Cost trade-off accepted: fact_extraction nightly stays on Haiku (~$10/night) while supersession + derivation moved to Gemini primary (saved ~$10/night via the v1.2.3.x refactor). Net post-refactor expected: ~$5-8/night, down from $20.

This decision is data-driven (this comparison) rather than assumption-driven, and should be re-validated when:
- A new model version is released (Haiku 5, Gemini Flash 3, or equivalent)
- The chunk content distribution shifts substantially (e.g. >30% content in a language other than Italian)
- The extraction prompt changes meaningfully
